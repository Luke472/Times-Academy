The journey of english speaking course to spoken english course was not organic

Beyond any shred of doubt, changes are permanent part of every industry ever in existence. Perhaps it only keeps things alive. Some organic changes that naturally come influence the industry was quite significant that still continues in it.

The english speaking course was the only prevalent term used till the late. But gradually the term 'Spoken English' came to use thereafter. It was first introduced by Rajesh Jaitely in 1996. Today, this term is being used throughout nation.

India's first IELTS teacher

TOEFL and IELTS (the previous name was EPTB; English Proficiency Test Battery) came into being in 1962 and 1963 respectively. In 1980, EPTB was replaced by ELTS (English Language Testing System) which was subsequently revised as IELTS in 1996.

Its training was offered in British Council Division (BCD). The first private institution which started training people in India for the same was established by Rajesh Jaitely in Jalandhar. As such, he possesses a vast bundle of experience which he endows on his students and ensures their success.
